About the Festival

Yugadi / Ugadi

ಯುಗಾದಿ ಹಬ್ಬ • ಹೊಸ ವರ್ಷ

Yugadi marks the beginning of the new year in the Hindu lunisolar calendar, celebrated with great fervor across Karnataka. The festival heralds the arrival of spring and new beginnings. Families gather to savor the iconic Bevu-Bella (a mix of neem and jaggery), symbolizing life's blend of bitter and sweet experiences. The day features Panchanga Shravana (reading of the new almanac), rangoli, cultural performances, and a grand festive feast.

Day 2 Tradition

Badoota

ಬಾಡೂಓ

Badoota (also known as Non-Veg feast day) is a beloved tradition observed the day after Yugadi in many Karnataka households. After the sacred vegetarian observance of Yugadi, families and friends come together for a hearty non-vegetarian feast featuring classic Karnataka dishes. It is a joyful, informal gathering that celebrates community, togetherness, and the rich culinary heritage of Karnataka.
